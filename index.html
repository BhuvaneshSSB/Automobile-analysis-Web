Description

The automobile inspection dataset provides crucial insights into the inspection activities conducted on vehicles, offering valuable information for analysis and decision-making. The dataset comprises various parameters, including the total number of vehicles inspected, the frequency of inspections, detection statistics for different vehicle parts, and trends in inspection activities across different days of the week.

Insights

  1. Total Vehicle Inspections:

    The dataset encompasses records of 44 vehicles that have undergone inspection procedures.

  2. Inspection Frequency:

    A total of 2388 inspections have been conducted across all vehicles in the dataset.

  3. Part Detection Statistics:

    The analysis reveals that the right front door part is the most frequently detected, with a total of 18194 detections. In contrast, the left Outside Rear View Mirror (ORWM) is the least detected part, with only 2315 detections.

  4. Vehicle Inspection Frequency:

    The vehicle with the highest number of inspections is identified as ABD19803, undergoing inspection procedures 25 times. Conversely, ABD67897 is the least inspected vehicle, with only one recorded inspection.

  5. Weekly Inspection Trends:

    Examination of the dataset indicates a notable drop in inspection counts on Saturdays. A recurring pattern is observed across most weeks, characterized by an increase in inspections from Monday to Tuesday, followed by a gradual decline on Wednesdays and Thursdays, with a subsequent rise on Fridays.
